Technical Drawing

Fundamentals of engineering drawing using AutoCAD. Covers multiview orthographic projection, section views, dimensioning and tolerancing per drafting standards, and the full workflow from freehand sketch to finished CAD drawing.

Key Takeaways

What I Learned

Multiview orthographic projection — constructing and reading top, front, and right-side views of 3D objects following third-angle projection convention.

Dimensioning and tolerancing — applying dimensions, tolerances, and geometric callouts according to engineering drafting standards.

Section views — producing full and half section drawings to reveal interior features that orthographic views cannot show clearly.

AutoCAD workflow — setting up layers, linetypes, and title blocks; using precision drawing tools; and managing drawing files to professional standards.

Sketch-to-CAD pipeline — moving from a freehand concept sketch through to a fully dimensioned, print-ready AutoCAD drawing.

Course Overview

About the Course

Technical Drawing introduces the language that engineers use to communicate design intent. Every manufactured part starts as a drawing — knowing how to read and produce one correctly is a fundamental engineering skill.

The course covers both the theory of projection (how a 3D object maps onto 2D views) and the practical tool skills needed to execute drawings in AutoCAD. Assignments progress from simple geometric shapes to more complex machined parts including angle brackets with full dimensioning.

The skills developed here feed directly into later courses — any time a design needs to be fabricated or communicated, it goes through a drawing.
